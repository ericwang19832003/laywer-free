# UX Copy Style Guide

## Tone

Warm, calming, confidence-building. Users are anxious self-represented litigants. Every word should make them feel supported, not overwhelmed.

## Approved Phrases

### Encouragement
- "One step at a time."
- "You're in control."
- "We'll guide the process and track deadlines."
- "Take your time — there's no rush."
- "You can update this anytime."
- "Nice work!"
- "You're all caught up."

### Next Steps
- "A common next step is..."
- "This helps us organize your documents and timeline."
- "We'll help you stay on top of every important date."

### Deadlines
- "Got it. We'll remind you ahead of time."
- "Coming up soon" (for near deadlines — NOT "overdue" or "urgent")

### Empty States
- "No cases yet. Let's get started — one step at a time."
- "No deadlines yet. That's okay — we'll add them as your case develops."
- "No activity yet. Complete your first step to see it here."
- "You're all caught up. Nice work!"

## Forbidden Phrases

### Legal Imperatives (we are NOT giving legal advice)
- "You must"
- "You are required to"
- "Failure to comply"
- "The law requires"
- "You should" (use "A common next step is..." instead)

### Scary/Urgent Language
- "URGENT"
- "WARNING"
- "ERROR"
- "FAILED"
- "OVERDUE"
- "ASAP"
- "IMMEDIATELY"
- "CRITICAL"
- "DANGER"

### Negative/Cold Language
- "Invalid"
- "Incorrect"
- "Wrong"
- "You forgot to..."
- "Missing required..."

## Color Rules

- **Never use red.** For any urgency, use warm amber (#D97706).
- Completed items: calming green (#16A34A)
- Primary actions: warm indigo (#4F46E5)
- Background: warm white (#FAFAF8)
- Text: warm charcoal (#1C1917)
- Muted text: warm stone (#78716C)

## Button Labels

- Primary CTA: "Review & Continue" (dashboard), "Confirm & Continue" (review step), "I'm ready" (welcome)
- Secondary: "Save and come back later", "Add a deadline"
- Never: "Submit", "Delete", "Cancel"

## Error Messages

Instead of: "Invalid email address"
Use: "That doesn't look like an email address. Could you double-check?"

Instead of: "Required field"
Use: "We need this to continue."

Instead of: "Error creating case"
Use: "Something went wrong. Let's try again."

## Disclaimer

Every page must include:
> "This tool provides general legal information and organization — not legal advice. For legal advice, please consult a licensed attorney."
